Context

For this type of analysis the important difference between a VPS and cloud is quite minor. In other contexts, a cloud is a more vaguely defined and may have less features. A VPS 1 is specifically a full virtual server, with no public IP.

old Q: Why should a business use a cloud or VPS (vs an on-prem option)?
A: To amortise costs across time and to get seamless upgrades; with support at higher levels than may be locally feasible.

Q: Which is more useful, a plain VPS / VM or multiple specific services?
A: If you are starting from fresh, the separate services are cheaper to run; but don't map well to older software. Rewriting a whole product is a daunting expense; with marginal gains. Small steps can be taken, like moving the DB to the specific service for biggest cost change.

Note: some industries benefit from all the computation being done in the same national borders where the founders live and advertise. Aside from legal and commercial concerns; having the servers physically nearer you or your customers' means there is generally a better connection speed (someone else's opinion on geo-location 2 ). The reason for this rewrite is to update and add further locales to this list.
Care about which countries data handling laws are being applied to your clients' data is important; and outside the scope of this technical article (also this will change for each starting location). UK government notes 3. See [book1] for discussion on this problem if your company is the size of Microsoft.
I observe that Amazon is frequently NOT the cheapest retailer on the slightly obscure stock in its online shops. I assume the same business practice of optimising for the most common sale applies to SaaS. Many of these VPS items 4 are labelled “public cloud”; I guess an architect would call the VMs running inside company firewalls a “private cloud”. The public options can be firewalled into isolation.
There are some companies that claim to sell in a given country; by which they mean they have a bank account in that place but no other facility, and resell a larger vendors products. This is not very useful for the buyer.

I advise looking at each vendor at point of purchase, as pricing will change since I wrote this.

Metrics/ Values

I have been in teams doing global deployment multiple times. This article makes me see the difference between global deployment and services in each country.

Whats important for selecting a VPS / “full VM” option:
  • Proximity of server real hardware to the client machine. Use of caching can increase useful distance.
  • Service uptime (needs to be very high percentage). I note that GCP down prioritises VPS that aren't being used over long weekends, and traffic will be a lot delayed for 5min on the first morning after. This isn't technically a service outage; but is annoying. I would assume other cloud providers have the same effect.
  • Business process (e.g. billing, support, documentation's locale) running in a locale that works for the client.
  • Cost, the product being sold is a commodity. Billing in local currency is good for clients, as it side steps fluctuations on the ForEx.
  • Access to 'good' fixed IPs; which are not used by spammers or scammers. This is a social engineering item.
  • Awareness of local legislation and features to support it.
