Definition of test success

To deliver 80% of required features, at less than 80% of budget, with 99% availability.
Then use the 20% for the things they didn't mention at the start.

quote Scrum: Deliver value 29 30.

So . . . time to negotiate?
“there ain't no such thing as a free lunch” 31 32

  • Assuming a normal e-commerce scenario, the business will care most about the most used and most visible user journeys.
  • Assuming financial services, the business will care the most just before an audit.
  • If you do something like blackfriday sales, it is business critical to have stress testing done before this event.
  • Assuming I am me, the highest value section is the DB. I can fix anything else fairly quickly; I can replace anything else at fairly low risk.

Heuristics, analysis is valuable for fast or complex projects

  • understand objectives before writing anything §
  • if a post-it notes meets supplied objectives, submit a post-it note; and go on holiday (this is cheap and fast, and stable)
  • label things correctly and clearly (i.e. setters must have Cyclomatic complexity / CC 41 42 43 of 1)
  • write tests to these objectives. These tests save you time thinking, thinking is harder than writing tests.
  • engage on management of process, to hold onto funds for deliverables
  • all points must cope with things moving.
    • text + git > docbook + git > msword + dropbox > email > msword + email
  • use spellcheck, see Drupal 44
  • yadadadadada

§ the faster you need to move, the more percentage of budget on understanding, as you have less room for error

Heuristics, control your solution generation

  • use types constraints wherever possible (see php 7.4 rfc )
  • do not write tests on methods with a CC of 1
  • in terms of testing, collection.forEach() > for loops > nested if statements
  • statelessness 45 reduces the amount of tests needed, but cannot be globally applied
  • DI 46 is very valuable
  • avoid long method definitions
  • avoid too many clauses per line
