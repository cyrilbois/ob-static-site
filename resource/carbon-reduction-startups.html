This is a list of startups, in London, UK; who have the started aim of reducing carbon footprint for a named business sector. I made this list as this is a new and expanding market. Please check last update time-stamp on this article in particular.
I don't do articles of lists of companies, as that is very dull content. I wrote this list as I didn't notice “green tech” being a job sector that got attention from investment people in 2015 or earlier. Yes, individual people and companies did things, e.g. the Princes Trust, or the Bodyshop, or renewable power suppliers; but there was no advertisement for the entire category of systematic investments.
I consider global warming is a massive economy damaging and life expectancy damaging issue. It is affecting many different systems that humans have been benefiting from for the last thousand plus years (e.g. the Atlantic Meridional overturning circulation 1 ). The planet Earth is a closed loop ~ due to gravity ~ extraction of any singular thing without replacement will lead to exhaustion of it (example Helium 2 ), inescapably. Until recently there have been very few businesses that operated as though this is a known long-term consequence and existential risk. We have no planet B, and we will soon have no “planet safety systems” (e.g. Mangroove swamps as a good carbon sink and wave breaker/ storm blocker 3, but being replaced with low density housing in the US, or shrimp farms in Asia) as we are destroying them. As an example of recent eco-change 4 there is now less ice in each pole each summer than previous years for geological time. The side effect of this ice-sheet collapse is a projected 3m rise in sea levels in less than a decade.

I am not a fervent believer in anything. This situation is an existential risk, and the fact it will affect your balance sheet negatively doesn't mean it's made up. There is a lot of hype about investment in carbon credits, and things that look like carbon reduction e.g. 5, but don't in-practice work. The link I included showed up in my socials earlier today, I don't know if there are better examples at present. BUT on balance, having the investment category is “lessWrong” than not having it.

Example A of critical failure: rising sea levels flood your stock market and many of your banks (specifically the data-centres, for example in CanaryWharf, which is a few meters above sea level).
Example B of critical failure: water cycles oscillating between drought and flood making current road or rail based networks break every year. This leads to supply chain failure across entire areas, and suspension of trade for any business in that area. Drinking water in that area would need to be delivered in sealed containers, as the existing drinking-water systems would be now polluted.
Example C of critical failure: extreme heat for months in the local summer, leading to electric distribution systems failing, as they were built for a colder climate northern Europe, and also increased fire risk.

Some of these companies are a B-corp. This means they are established enough to have completed the Bcorp paperwork process.

Startups that plant trees/ improve biosphere

  • Ecologi ~ This is a carbon offset company by planting trees.
  • GrowUp Farms ~ Vertical farms. UK production, as limited land use; and also many less food miles as farm is near consumer. They have strict and controlled process, so less insecticides etc.
  • Treeapp ~ A B-corp who organises tree planting, internationally. Sells the carbon credit to other companies.
  • Alvátech ~ Agri-tech, water management. Reduce average salinity in water sources. Global footprint, office in the UK.
  • Mondra ~ Online greengrocer using some ML modeling and trying for carbon neutral food production.
  • Green talk ~ Platform for logging and displaying biodiversity. To end users, is a data system. Not sure if I should remove.
  • Bageera ~ Company delivers land enhancement and a finance platform for investment in land, They claim an average return a better rate than many stocks.
  • Oddbox ~ Organisation to get then resell veg that supermarkets wouldn't buy as misshapen. Delivery to end-user rather than shop.
  • Terraprima do not confuse with terraprima.pt
  • BX ~ A company performing data aggregation, collection, certification relating to carbon related paperwork, about food. Is expanding sales on a per-country basis; but has a UK limited company.
  • Better Origin ~ Company re-creates sections of the ecosphere that modern farming destroys. Claims to reduce carbon emissions by keeping it in the bio-web, but it in other animals to tradition. I am cheating abit as this is a Cambridge company.

Startups that do transport, EV/ trains

  • Charge point ~ A platform to allow all car/ EV charging-points to be compatible. These also have a foot in the US.
  • Chill-chain ~ A startup with a management panel to ensure that a companies trucks get used more efficiently, by removing empty return trips, and better data and monitoring. This reduces the clients running costs, and the average amount of traffic on the roads; and so reduces pollution/ carbon footprint.
  • Pod Point ~ EV charging.
  • Urban Electric Networks ~ Creates reclining EV charge points.
  • ALP Technologies ~ Research and battery technology.
  • [MyCarGoes] ~ no website, 1y old, has domain for email, is on LI.
  • Plasma Track ~ Service to make train tracks safer and able to take faster trains. Uses plasma, and I assume a honking battery.

Selected Interventions ~ "ECHO™ Service Management Framework software”, looks like its Jira for binmen.

  • Hived ~ An EV-only delivery/courier company, has good scores on Trustpilot.

Startups that do packaging

  • EnvoPAP ~ Creates packaging from non-traditional sources, to reduce deforestation.
  • London Bio Packaging ~ Either re-used packaging, or compostable packaging.
  • Notpla ~ Quote “make packaging disappear”, products aiming to phase out single use plastic.
  • Vesta Smart Packaging ~ As the name says, reduction in waste by better IoT and refill shipping.
  • Flexi-hex ~ Sells packaging based on cardboard, so is recyclable. This eliminates plastic.
  • Decent packaging ~ Sells packaging for hospitality, but without use-once plastic. Packaging items made from plants.

Startups that do power tech/ solar

  • Open Climate Fix ~ A non-profit who create models and aggregate data to use existing systems better. Also organising rollouts for solar power.
  • Renbee ~ This is a company managing paperwork for renewables installation (e.g. solar panels).
  • MiniBEMS 1 ~ When you have a “heat network”, miniBEMs is turbocharged version. Mostly better telemetry, and heat exchange units.
  • East London Biogas 2 ~ This site has problems with Cloudflare.
  • Bulb ~ A UK based vendor who only sell green/ renewable energy for domestic and office end-users. Bought by Octopus energy who claim to be as good.
  • Low carbon ~ Low carbon energy suppler, claims to service about 400,000 homes. Also running Funds.
  • look at EDF

Startups for greener buildings/ proptech

  • Cercula ~ Own “life cycle software” to reduce carbon used in building/ construction. The advertising is talking about changing building design.
  • N-House ~ Modular property design & construction, [not sure if ecotech or not].
  • Plentific ~ Is available in EN and DE, and is a property management platform. They are relevant to this list, as they assist with carbon neutral refits on buildings.
  • Common VC ~ An incubator and basic office facility, focussed on social challenge startups.
  • Green Pixie ~ A startup to reduce carbon created by running cloud data centres.

Startups for waste

  • Xworks Tech ~ A “waste trading” platform, marked up like a stock market.
  • Carbon Catchers 3 ~ Sells a thing to catch and consume CO2 from air.
  • Scindo ~ They create enzymes for industrial recycling, consuming leaked crude oil and plastic in landfill.
  • Ocean bottle ~ Startup the organises removal of plastic waste from the ocean, also sells expensive water bottles.
  • 4 ocean ~ Operates like a charity, organises plastic extraction from the ocean. Does have a UK entity, but is based in Florida.
  • The first mile ~ Improve corporate recycling and sustainability; improve circular economy. Collect waste without using ICE vehicles.

Startups for supply chain

  • Sweep ~ Do supply chain analysis and ecological certification.
  • Circulor ~ Services supply chain traceability.
  • Hubbub UK ~ Organisation/ platform for circular economy & reuse process. Brochure is focussed on end user items. Runs a phone reuse scheme.
  • Cervest ~ Owns “earthscan” which reports risks to assets, I assume buildings. They mention that also do reports for manufacturing process, focussed on supply-chain; and same for governments.

“green recruiters”

  • Storm4 ~ A green-tech recruiter.
  • Areti Group ~ Claims to be a carbon positive be recruiter; I hope that means there is less carbon after they operate.
  • Eco Careers ~ This looks like a scrape site, but has some relevant JD for ecological-friendly roles.

Paperwork: carbon reporting/ risk charting/ finance/ trading

  • BeZero Carbon ~ Built and run a platform for trading carbon emissions/ permits/ offsets / sinks.
  • Abatable ~ Carbon offset management service. They note many companies have carbon avoidance, rather than carbon removal process. Abatable wish to manage this better.
  • CarbonChain ~ Supplier for a dashboard to manage and track current carbon status for a company.
  • Zeigo ~ Is a climate-tech platform enabling companies to access renewable energy.
  • Zevero ~ This is a platform to measure and monitor carbon footprint.
  • SparkChange ~ Fintech, with Carbon features, claim focussed on netzero goal.
  • Kita ~ “Carbon insurance”, claim connection is big UK insurance, inside FCA.
  • Earth Capital ~ Investment manager with ESG. Mention portfolio in energy, food.
  • Redigo ~ Cloud-based sustainability platform, with scenario planner. Caution, on 2023-06-04 template footer contains a spelling error (this is a finance platform, not a FB post, or a blog article)..
  • Global Optimism ~ Meta organisation for “achieving net-zero by 2040”, externally looks like mailing list hosted by AWS (an early signup in 2019).
  • Eco connect ~ Organisation for permatech and long term, low impact dwellings. This seems expensive in land... organisationally a community website and news distrib platform.
  • GEP Environmental ~ Paperwork people, loads of paperwork: Carbon data/ tracking capture, UKG paperwork, energy certs, airport carbon paperwork, etc.
  • Altruistiq 4 ~ Data platform for carbon tracking. Many photos of tree planting.
  • Carbon RE 5 ~ Are targeting very dirty industries, claim to have an AI product to knock 10per off production costs; also cross-sells alternative energy suppliers.
  • Emitwise 6 ~ Carbon accounting for auto, FMCG, buildings and packaging. Claim to reduce risk.
  • Earthly ~ Quote “Remove carbon. Restore nature. Improve livelihoods” but more accurately I think “right science-backed nature-based projects”.
  • Climate impact 7 8 ~ Sells paper-trails for sustainability projects mostly to areas with less infrastructure.
  • ecosphere+ ~ Sell carbon credits, front page says it has been bought by Abatable.
  • Future proof ~ A Netzero ESG.
  • Green element ~ A company that seems to sell to other Bcorps, maybe to achieve Bcorp status. Has a carbon tracking tool & other paperwork.
