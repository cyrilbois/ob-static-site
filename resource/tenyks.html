Dear reader,
I am a senior software engineer, who has a focus in UX as it improves product fit, and is very valuable making reporting on analytics products useful. I live in London, UK, and am looking for a new role. I believe I am useful to your company as:

  • At Hoptroff, a startup selling financial compliance tools, I spent five years building 'better graphs' on top of D3. I wrote a wrapper to supply the “advanced graph features” that my Phd enabled CTO ~ “chief time officer” required. In 2021 I went to a data-vis conference. For interaction design, I would talk longer about my time at Swyvl, where I massively improved the user-journeys of the first iteration. I care that I deliver value to my users; that the tools that I supply are clear and make sense inside the problem domain. I value when I can make an interaction which is very simple and intuitive to users, but correctly performs a complex business process.
  • At Hoptroff, the data was hosted in MongoDB; I did the initial devops to establish the UK and test clusters. I spent some time improving 'data cleanness', rendering a graph of a useful extraction/summary of twenty million documents/ month in a second. After the second year of me working there, we had a dedicated devop engineer.
  • I have built REST API in Python for a several years, across two employers (Hoptroff, and MotorTRAK). I also have many years experience building REST API in Golang, PHP and Perl.
  • At Hoptroff I built two products, and extended other software. I have been officially a lead engineer at Venue10, but also been “most senior frontend engineer” at Hoptroff and Swyvl. When doing this, about 30% of my time was organising sprints and defining what “Done” meant. I have consulted with clients to support this.
  • As is common in web services, I tend towards CI/CD; and incremental releases. When requirements seem wobbly, I organise a very cheap solution that clearly demonstrates the user journey (e.g. an interaction to get reporting, and where the focus was on time inputs; the interaction generated a report, but technically the same static fixture report with “TEST DATA” across the top). I can demo several UI prototype to reviewer audiences easily by changing git branch. Quite often this type of review is inside the dev team, and looking at the user journey on a wide range of devices.
  • Alternatively for actual external clients, I would try to get a 'user interview' showing a full feature iteration. The interview is to get what the users want to achieve, and what their constraints are.
  • I have wide experience with computer graphics, although this isn't represented in my CV aside from webapp and website creation. During my first degree (graduated in 2001), I lived next to a Phd student doing a face detection thesis, a feature now present in most phones. I have used Edge detection algorithms but that is the closest to computer comprehension of vision that I have built. I have integrated live/ real time video feeds into websites. In theory I can draw overlays on a video feed inside the browser, but I would test that carefully.
  • I have deployed services to AWS and google cloud.

I hope to hear from you soon.
Owen Beresford
